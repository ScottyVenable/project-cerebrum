# Research Paper: Neural Sovereignty and the Ethical BCI
**Author:** Fathom Research Division
**Date:** May 2026

## Abstract
As Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI) transition from clinical settings to the consumer market, the risks of "mental surveillance" and "neuro-capitalism" become acute. This paper outlines the ethical imperative for "Local-First" BCI architectures and defines the framework of Neural Sovereignty.

## 1. The Threat of Centralized Neuro-Data
Centralized brain-data repositories represent an unprecedented risk to human privacy. Unlike biometric data, neural signals contain the seeds of subconscious bias, emotional state, and future intent.

## 2. Neural Sovereignty Defined
Neural Sovereignty is the fundamental right of an individual to exercise absolute control over their own cognitive and neural data. This includes:
- **Right to Local Processing:** Neural signals must be decoded on user-owned hardware.
- **Right to Disconnection:** The ability to physically and digitally sever the BCI link without data residue.

## 3. The Cerebrum Approach
Project Cerebrum implements these ethics through:
- **Edge Inference:** All EEGNet models run on a local Raspberry Pi 5.
- **Ephemeral Data Policy:** Raw signals are processed in volatile memory and purged immediately after command execution.

## 4. Conclusion
True human-machine integration requires a foundation of absolute trust. By open-sourcing the hardware and software pipeline, Project Cerebrum establishes a transparent standard for the future of ethical neurotechnology.
