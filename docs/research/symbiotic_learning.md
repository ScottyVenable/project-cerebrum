# Research Paper: Neurofeedback and the Symbiotic Learning Loop
**Author:** Fathom Research Division
**Date:** May 2026

## Abstract
This paper analyzes the "Symbiotic Learning Loop" in Brain-Computer Interfacing (BCI). We propose that BCI performance is not merely a product of AI decoding accuracy, but a mutual adaptation process where the user's sensorimotor cortex adapts to the AI's classification boundaries.

## 1. The Adaptive User
BCI "illiteracy" (the inability to control a BCI) is often a failure of the feedback loop. By providing high-fidelity, low-latency visual representation of neural stability (Alpha/Beta ratios), we trigger the brain's inherent neuroplasticity.

## 2. Neural Stability Index
We implement a "Stability Index" derived from real-time Power Spectral Density (PSD). This index provides the user with an immediate "gauge" of their mental focus, enabling them to consciously suppress noise and enhance motor imagery signals.

## 3. Mutual Reinforcement
As the user stabilizes their brain state, the EEGNet model receives cleaner training data, resulting in higher classification weights. This creates a virtuous cycle of increasing accuracy and reducing cognitive load.

## 4. Conclusion
Project Cerebrum's integration of real-time stability visualization transforms the BCI from a passive tool into a symbiotic extension of the user's nervous system.
